What is a Landing Page?

A landing page is a web page focused on something specific, like an offer, an ebook, a course, a discount, etc. It has a form, a button, a title, an image or video, and a clear and compelling value proposal. The goal of a landing page is to convert visitors into leads or customers by capturing their information or driving them to take action. A well-designed landing page can generate more results for a business.

Top 3 things

You can't lack...

A form to capture visitors' contact information, such as name, email, phone, etc.

A call-to-action button that encourages visitors to fill out the form or perform another desired action such as purchasing, signing up, downloading, etc. The button must have clear and attractive text, such as “I want my free ebook”, “Secure your spot now”, “Take advantage of the discount”, etc.

A value proposition that explains the benefits of the offer to visitors, such as what they will learn, gain, save, solve, etc. The value proposition must be brief, specific, and convincing, such as “Learn how to create incredible landing pages in 10 simple steps”, “Get a free month of unlimited access to our online course platform”, “Save up to 50% on your purchase of selected products”, etc.

Great Design

It is important that a design has...

Organization

The organization of elements on the page should make it easier to read and understand the main message. The most important elements, such as the logo, title, and image, should be visible at the top of the page, without the need to scroll.

Harmony

The choice of colors, fonts, images and icons must follow a coherent visual identity aligned with the brand. The colors should contrast well with each other and with the background, to highlight important elements and facilitate reading. Fonts must be legible and appropriate to the tone of the message.

The content

The landing page text must be clear, concise and convincing to capture the visitor's attention and interest. The title should summarize the value of the offer and arouse curiosity. The main message must explain the benefits and advantages of the offer, using simple and direct language. The CTA button must be eye-catching and action-oriented, using imperative verbs.
